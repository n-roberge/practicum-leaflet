A Growing Global Challenge

As climate change continues to strain nations, poorer countries are disproportionately effected by food and water security. Regions that depend on rain-fed agriculture will be particularly vulnerable in the next 20 years.

Droughts are estimated to be the costliest and most far-reaching natural disasters. Drought management and risk-based approaches to disaster management will be paramount in reducing vulnerability to droughts in the future.

In the Horn of Africa alone, 20 million people are facing famine due to drought. In Ethiopia, over 7 million are without sustainable food. And up to 30% of households' herds have died in Somalila in the past year.

The aim of this project is to provide some awareness on initial areas of future study that are of particular interest to stakeholders in water security in Africa.

Potentially Susceptible Areas in Africa

Mapping Water Security Vulnerability

Predicting where conflict may occur is not a hard science. There are a number of factors that could potentially be involved, but this map provides an initial look at where these areas may begin. In this map, crops that could potentially fail due to drought are highlighted. Site selection was based on poverty (measured by infant mortality rate (IMR)), abundance of rainfed crops, and potential drought. Armed conflict and location data (ACLED) has also been included to give greater situational awareness
